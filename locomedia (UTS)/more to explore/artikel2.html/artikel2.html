List of Ethnic Groups in Indonesia and Social Institutions of Its People

Indonesia consists of about 1,340 ethnic groups spread almost throughout Indonesia. According to BPS data itself half or 50% of the nation's tribe in the country is Javanese. The rest of the tribes that inhabit the territory of Indonesia outside Java such as the Makasar Bugis tribe (3.68%), Batak 2.04%, Bali 1.88%, Aceh 1.4%, and other tribes. Each tribe has different customs and norms. However, diversity does not make the nation fragmented, instead diversity then unites to achieve the goal of a just and prosperous society.

Understanding the Nation

A tribe is a class of people who identify with others based on lineage referring to characteristics such as culture, nation, language, religion and behavior. Ethnic groups are also a social group that is distinguished from other social groups, because it has the most basic and common characteristics related to its origin, place of origin, and culture. In another definition, a tribe is also a special social tribe and is asscriptive or has existed since birth, and has the same pattern as the age and gender. The tribes themselves can be grouped based on: Mixed tribes, in which there is a mixture between races that inhabit one particular region or region. For example, in the Peranakan tribe which is a mixture between Chinese and Malay races. Lineage, as the main factor for the tribe. There are three lineages in Indonesia, namely the paternal lineage (patrilineal), usually in batak, Ambon, Timor and others, maternal lineage (matrilineal), usually occurs in the Minangkabau tribe in South Sumatra and the paternal and maternal lineage that is widely run by Javanese tribes.

Suku Jawa

Javanese people using The Javanese language itself have different rules in terms of vocabulary and intonation based on the relationship between the speaker and the interlocutor, known as upload-ungguh. This aspect of language has a strong social influence in Javanese culture, and makes them very aware of their social status in society. In Javanese society, the kinship system is based on bilateral lineages (calculated from two sides, mother and father). With this bilateral or parental principle, a Javanese is in touch as extensively as the family on the part of the mother and also the father. Relatively solid kinship is usually entwined in the offspring of one ancestor up to the third generation. However, the quality of nuclear family and extended family relationships varies from family to family, depending on the condition of each family.

Suku Sunda

Sundanese people known as Pasundan Tatars cover the western part of java island where most of the territory goes into the provinces of West Java and Banten. It comes from the root of the word sunda or suddha in Sanskrit which means shining, bright and white. Sundanese themselves make up 5.5 percent of indonesia's total population as a whole. Although spread across various regions of Indonesia, but most sundanese people occupy banten, Jakarta, and Java. The majority of this tribe is Muslim but there is also a small percentage who are Christian, Hindu and even Sunda Wiwitan.

Suku Dayak

Dayak comes from the word "Daya" which means upstream, to refer to people who live in the interior or perhuluan. The Dayak tribe itself is one of the "Asli" tribes that inhabit the "Island of Borneo" (Kalimantan). Borneo is divided based on administrative regions which each consist of East Kalimantan capital Samarinda, South Kalimantan capital Banjarmasin, Central Kalimantan capital Palangka Raya, West Kalimantan capital Pontianak, and North Kalimantan capital Tanjung Selor. Dayak tribe has similar cultural characteristics that are typical, among others such as mandau, chopsticks, beliong, betang house or long house (radank house) and others. Other dayak characteristics such as; gun ownership, and its cultural arts. The original dayak kaharingan religion is an indigenous religion born from the culture of the ancestors. Some Dayak people still hold firm belief in the existence of magical objects in certain places such as rocks, large trees, parks in forests, lakes, lubuk, and others that according to the belief have the "supernatural power" of Jubata and Batara. Currently, the number of Dayak people is about 1.27 percent of the total population of Indonesia as a whole.

Suku Asmat

Known as the tribe of the drops of Gods, this Tribe of Papuan origin believes that they come from the descendants of Dewa Fumeripits. The Asmat tribe is also one of the tribes of Papua Province that is worldwide because of its culture that is so respectful of nature and the lives of its ancestors, so the wisdom possessed by the Asmat tribe is also very extraordinary. This one ethnicity is divided into two, namely tribes that live on the coast and tribes that live in the interior. The two populations differ in many aspects such as from way of life, dialect, ritual, even social structure. The division of the Asmat language downstream of the river is divided into part of the northwest coast group and part of the southwest coast group. While the division of the upstream Asmat language is divided into groups Keenok and Kaimok.

Suku Sumbawa

The Sumbawa tribe originated in Sumbawa, West Nusa Tenggara. Judging from its geographical location, Sumbawa is in an area that has good forest products because it is in the hills. Some of them such as teak, rattan, sepang wood, menjangan, and honey. Sumbawa people inhabit the kabutapen areas of Sumbawa and West Sumbawa. The majority of the religion of sumbawa people is Islam, so many activities are carried out related to religion. But despite this, sumbawa people generally still believe in superstition. Such as superstitions about the surrounding environment, superstitions about the unseen realm, superstitions about the universe, and others.
