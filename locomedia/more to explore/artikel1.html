Danau Toba

What is the origin and history of Lake Toba? This morning's story will review it from several sources and expert research. This lake is located in the middle of North Sumatra province and is surrounded by seven districts namely, Samosir Regency, Simalungun, Humbang Hasundutan, Dairi, Karo and Toba Samosir, and North Tapanuli. This tekto-volcanic lake is the largest lake in Southeast Asia because it has a length of 87 kilometers, a width of 27 kilometers, a location of an altitude of 904 meters above sea level. And the depth reaches 505 meters. Geographically, Lake Toba is at coordinates 980,300 to 990,010 East Longitude and 20,240 to 20,480 North Latitude This lake is categorized as a wet tropical climate with climate types C to E, the temperature ranges from 170-290 C and the average air humidity is 85.04 percent. From Lake Toba, flows a large river that is the Asahan river. This river flows into the lowlands to the waters of the Strait of Malacca in the east of sumatra island. The rapid flow of this rivger is eventually used by the government as a hydroelectric power plant (PLTA). According to history, Lake Toba was previously a volcano called Mount Toba. Information quoted from sejarahlengkap.com mentioned, this mountain has a very large magma pocket that if erupted will produce a very high explosive power. The magma pockets of Mount Toba are supplied by the abundance of melt a number of melts of a hyperactive continental plate, namely the Indo-Australian plate that contains a lot of sediment, and the Eurasian plate that seats sumatra island. The location of the two plates is at a depth of 150 km below the earth. Friction of the Indo-Australian and Eurasian plates generates heat so that it melts the rocks. The melt then rises to the top as magma. Because of the frequent rubbing of these two plates, the magma produced is large enough to create such a powerful explosion.

Taman nasional way kambas

Way Kambas National Park is the first and oldest National Park in Indonesia, is one of the educational attractions open to the wider community. Way kambas is the site of the first elephant training center in Indonesia. The early establishment of this National Park aimed to protect the existence of elephants and at the same time create mutual benefit for both elephants and humans. Sumatran elephants have long been an important part of the people of Sumatra Island. Even in ancient times, elephants were used to strengthen the war forces in the sultanate era. Way kambas National Park is an elephant protection national park located in lampung area precisely Labuhan Ratu subdistrict, East Lampung, Indonesia. In 1924 the Forest Area of Way Kambas (East Lampung) and branches (Central Lampung) were set aside as protected forest areas, together with the forest areas incorporated in it. Way Kambas National Park is a representative of lowland forest ecosystems that stand from lowland forests that stand from freshwater swamp forests, reed fields and Sumatran coastal forests.
